A resistor on a bus

A source pushes current down the bus, through a resistor, and back to the return. The resistor fights that flow, and the work it loses to heat is P = I²R — so the glow follows the square of the current. Double the current and the heat quadruples. Push the CURRENT slider, watch V = I·R rise in lockstep, and hit BOOST to slam it to the rail.
