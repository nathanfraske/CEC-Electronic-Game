The one-way valve

An AC source swings the bus up and down. A diode is a one-way valve: it lets the forward half through to the load and slams shut on the reverse half, so the carriers stall against a closed gate. What gets through is a half-wave — bumps of one polarity with gaps between. Add the smoothing cap and those gaps fill in toward a rough DC rail. The valve also costs about 0.6 V to open, so the output sits a diode-drop below the peak.
