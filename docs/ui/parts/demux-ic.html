The 1:2 demultiplexer, five layers down

A demultiplexer is the mirror of a multiplexer: it steers one data input to one of several outputs. This is the house part CEC2032, a Critical Error Computing 1-to-2 teaching chip whose input D appears at Y0 when the select S is low and at Y1 when S is high, with the unselected output held low. As a Boolean expression that is Y0 = S' AND D and Y1 = S AND D, so the data reaches exactly one output at a time. The standard build is one inverter on the select and two two-input AND gates, with no combining gate. The first tier is the logic symbol on the real pinout with the four-row truth table. The second is the flow network, a diverter that throws under select pressure to send D to one outlet or the other. The third opens that as pressure-pilot valves, two branches each a select valve over the shared data valve, feeding the outputs separately. The fourth is the gate-level schematic, the three real gates that are the device: the select inverter and the two AND gates. The fifth is the silicon, a representative gate of the set laid out as its CMOS transistors on one die.
