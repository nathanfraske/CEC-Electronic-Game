The MOSFET, three ways

A MOSFET is a voltage controlled switch. The gate sits behind a thin insulator and draws no current, it only sets up a field. Once the gate voltage clears the threshold a channel forms between drain and source and current flows, and the channel widens with more gate drive. Below threshold the channel is gone and the device is off. Switch tiers to see the same device as a schematic, as a pressure-piloted valve, and as the real silicon underneath.
