The resistor, across the three tiers

The same resistor shown three ways, switched with the tier control. Tier 1 is the schematic: the symbol on a wire, with current flowing through it and the potential dropping from the high side to the low side. Tier 2 is the reality-analogous view: water forced through a narrow channel, where the pressure drop is the voltage, the flow rate is the current, and the friction in the throat is the heat. Tier 3 is the reality-matched view: a cutaway of the conductor itself, with electrons drifting under the field and scattering off the vibrating lattice, which is what resistance and heating physically are. All three run on the same Ohm's law model, I = V / R with P = V² / R, and the resistance is a free parameter for the game to set.
