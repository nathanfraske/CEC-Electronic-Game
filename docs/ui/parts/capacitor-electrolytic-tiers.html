The capacitor, across the three tiers

The same capacitor shown three ways, switched with the tier control and wired as a charge and discharge loop. This one is an aluminum electrolytic, the big polarized workhorse used for bulk storage and smoothing. Tier 1 is the schematic: a supply, a series resistor, and the polarized capacitor symbol, with current that surges then fades as charge piles onto the plates. Tier 2 is the reality-analogous view: a tank filled through a valve, where the water level is the voltage, the stored water is the charge, and a wider tank is a larger capacitance. Tier 3 is the reality-matched view: a cutaway of the foil, where an etched aluminum anode carries a very thin oxide as the dielectric, a conductive electrolyte forms the other plate, electrons move in the metal while ions split in the electrolyte, and the oxide is what makes it polarized. All three run on one model, Q = C V with I = C dV/dt, the charge follows R C, and every value is a free parameter for the game to set.
