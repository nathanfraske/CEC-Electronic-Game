The diode building, and what it is doing

This is the factory building for the diode: a cutaway of the junction inside, not the schematic symbol. Current inside a diode is carried by charges moving through the crystal, and you are watching those carriers move in real time. The left contact is the anode, the right is the cathode, and the junction sits in the middle. Push the voltage forward and the carriers are driven into the junction where they cross and current flows; reverse it and they are pulled apart, the bare middle region widens into a barrier, and almost nothing gets through. The three types work by genuinely different physics, so each shows a different picture. Pick one and watch. Push the reverse voltage far enough and the junction breaks down.
