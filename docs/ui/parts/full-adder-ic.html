CEC2018S0 C0

The full adder, five layers down

A full adder adds three bits, two addends and a carry-in, and reports a sum and a carry-out. This is the house part CEC2018, a Critical Error Computing teaching chip that is the repeating cell of every ripple-carry adder. Two outputs fall out of the three inputs: the sum bit SUM is the parity of A, B and CIN, high when an odd number of the three are high, and the carry bit COUT is the majority, high when two or three of them are high. The standard build is two CEC2024 half adders and an OR: the first half adder sums A and B, the second sums that result with CIN to make SUM, and the two half-adder carries are OR-ed into COUT. The first tier is the logic symbol on the real pinout with the eight-row truth table. The second is the flow network, three input streams feeding a tally whose remainder is the sum and whose overflow is the carry. The third opens that as pressure-pilot valves, a parity valve that lifts on an odd count of pressurized lines and a majority valve that lifts once two of the three are pressurized. The fourth is the gate-level schematic, the five real gates that are the device: two XORs in series make SUM, two ANDs and an OR make COUT. The fifth is the silicon, a representative gate of the set laid out as its CMOS transistors on one die.
