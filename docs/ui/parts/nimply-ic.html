NIMPLYPULL-UP

The NIMPLY gate, five layers down

A NIMPLY gate, the negated conditional, is the complement of IMPLY: it gives high out in exactly one case, when A is true and B is false, and low everywhere else. It is the abjunction, "A but not B," which is just A AND NOT B, equivalently NOR(NOT A, B). Like IMPLY it inverts only one input, but here it is the mirror image: A passes through a small inverter to make NOT A, and that feeds a NOR with B straight in. Six transistors, one hidden node Ai, and the opposite asymmetry from IMPLY, since the gate is positive-unate in A but negative-unate in B. There is no discrete NIMPLY chip; in static CMOS it is just an inverter feeding a NOR, the cell shown here. It is the dual of IMPLY in the literal CMOS sense: where IMPLY used a series pull-down and parallel pull-up, NIMPLY uses a parallel pull-down and series pull-up, the same swap that turns a NAND into a NOR. The first tier is the logic symbol, an OR body with a bubble on the A input and a bubble on the output. The second is the gate as a flow network. The third opens it up as pressure-pilot valves. The fourth is the device as a schematic, the A inverter feeding the two-input NOR. The fifth is the silicon, the metal-oxide cross-section of all six devices on one die. Each input is a real voltage, so the logic shows at the ends and the switching event shows in the transfer curve.
