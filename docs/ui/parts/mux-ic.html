The 2:1 multiplexer, five layers down

A multiplexer routes one of several data lines to a single output. This is the house part CEC2031, a Critical Error Computing 2-to-1 teaching chip whose output Y follows D0 when the select S is low and D1 when S is high. As a Boolean expression that is Y = (S' AND D0) OR (S AND D1), so at any moment exactly one data input is connected through and the other is ignored. The standard build is one inverter on the select, two two-input AND gates used as pass gates, and a two-input OR. The first tier is the logic symbol on the real pinout with the eight-row truth table. The second is the flow network, a selector that throws a diverter under select pressure to connect Y to one input pipe or the other. The third opens that as pressure-pilot valves, two pass branches of valves in series, one gated by S and one by its complement, merging into the output. The fourth is the gate-level schematic, the four real gates that are the device: the select inverter, the two AND pass gates, and the OR. The fifth is the silicon, a representative gate of the set laid out as its CMOS transistors on one die.
