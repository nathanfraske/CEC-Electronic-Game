The majority gate, five layers down

A majority gate reports which way a three-bit vote went. This is the house part CEC2046, a Critical Error Computing teaching chip whose single output Y is the majority of A, B and C, high when two or three of the three inputs are high and low otherwise. As a Boolean expression that is Y = AB + BC + CA, the sum of the three pairwise products, which is the same carry-out function the CEC2018 full adder computes, sold here on its own. The standard build is three two-input AND gates, one per pair, feeding a three-input OR. The first tier is the logic symbol on the real pinout with the eight-row truth table. The second is the flow network, three input streams feeding a tally whose output opens once the count reaches two. The third opens that as a pressure-pilot valve, a majority valve that lifts only once two of the three pilot lines are pressurized. The fourth is the gate-level schematic, the four real gates that are the device: three ANDs on the input pairs and a three-input OR. The fifth is the silicon, a representative gate of the set laid out as its CMOS transistors on one die.
