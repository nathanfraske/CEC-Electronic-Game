CEC2057HI-Z

The tri-state buffer, five layers down

A buffer copies its input to its output, but the CEC2057 adds a third choice. When the output enable OE is high it drives Y to match A, a clean 0 or a clean VCC. When OE is low it releases Y entirely: not high, not low, just electrically disconnected from both rails. That released state is high-impedance, or Hi-Z, and it is the whole idea of a shared bus: many drivers on one wire, exactly one enabled at a time, the rest absent. Hi-Z has no waveform, so it is met as a concept, and the cleanest place to see it is at the transistor level, where it is simply both output transistors switched off so Y hangs on nothing. The first tier is the logic symbol on the real five-pin pinout with the function table. The second is the overall analogy, the output as a chamber that OE can cut off from both tanks. The third opens the output stage as two pressure-pilot valves, and when OE drops both shut. The fourth is the real device, the OE-gated CMOS output pair with its NAND, NOR, and inverter control. The fifth is the silicon. In place of a transfer curve, the panel at right is a shared bus: two of these drivers on one wire, where one drives while the other floats, and two at once collide.
