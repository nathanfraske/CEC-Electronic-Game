The NOR gate, five layers down

A NOR gate gives high out only when both inputs are low, and low out otherwise. Here it is one gate, the single 2-input NOR chip SN74LVC1G02, in a five-pin package with inputs A and B on pins 1 and 2. The first tier is the logic symbol with the real pinout. The second is the overall analogy, the gate as a flow network where a supply tank fills an output chamber and a drain empties it, with a pull-up built from two valves in series (both must open to fill) and a pull-down built from two valves in parallel (either one drains). The third opens those valves up as pressure-pilot valves, the working MOSFET analogy: a sealed gate line sets a spring-loaded plug. The fourth is the real device, the SOT-23-5 package and the CMOS network drawn as a schematic, two PMOS in series stacked over two NMOS in parallel. The fifth is the silicon, the metal-oxide cross-section of that network on one die. Each input is a real voltage, so the logic shows at the ends and the crossover shows in the transfer curve.
