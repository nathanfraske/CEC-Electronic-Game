The capacitor, across the three tiers

The same capacitor shown three ways, switched with the tier control and wired as a simple charge and discharge loop so you can watch it work. Tier 1 is the schematic: a supply, a series resistor, and the capacitor symbol, with current that surges then fades as charge piles onto the plates. Tier 2 is the reality-analogous view: a piston on a spring sealing a pipe, where the spring is the capacitor pushing back, the piston travel is the voltage, the swept water is the charge, and a taller pipe is a larger capacitance, with water flowing on both sides while nothing crosses. Tier 3 is the reality-matched view: a cutaway of the multilayer chip, where electrons gather on the interleaved electrodes and the ceramic dielectric polarizes while no charge ever crosses the gap, which is what storing charge physically means. All three run on one model, Q = C V with I = C dV/dt, the charge follows R C, and every value is a free parameter for the game to set.
