Varistor, three ways

A varistor is a resistor whose resistance depends on the voltage across it. Below its clamp voltage it is nearly an insulator and passes almost nothing. Above the clamp its resistance collapses and it conducts hard, which pins the voltage near the clamp and dumps the surplus current. It works the same in both directions, which is why it sits across the line in a surge protector. Switch tiers to see it as a schematic, as a pressure relief valve, and as the zinc oxide grains underneath.
