The bipolar transistor, three ways

A bipolar junction transistor is a current amplifier. A small base current sets a much larger collector current, with the ratio being the gain beta. The base to emitter junction is just a diode, the same check valve from before, so once it is forward biased near 0.7 V the device turns on and the collector current follows the base current. Switch tiers to see the same device as a schematic, as a hydraulic amplifier, and as the real silicon underneath. Switch type to flip between NPN and PNP.
