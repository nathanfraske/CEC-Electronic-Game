ZENER DIODEREVERSE CLAMP

The Zener diode, a check valve with a spillway

A Zener is an ordinary silicon diode in the forward direction and a controlled clamp in reverse. The forward path is the same spring loaded check valve as before, the ball lifting off its seat once the push beats the spring at about 0.7 V. The reverse path is a standpipe with a spillway, where the water level is the reverse voltage and the height of the spillway wall is the Zener voltage Vz. Push the bias negative and the level climbs until it reaches the wall, then it pours over and holds there, which is the clamp. Raise Vz to build the wall higher. Everything is driven by the real diode equation with a sharp reverse breakdown and a 100 ohm series limiter.
