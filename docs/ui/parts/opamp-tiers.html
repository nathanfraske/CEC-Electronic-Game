Op-amp, three ways

An operational amplifier watches two input voltages, V+ and V−, and drives its output to their difference multiplied by a large gain. Because the gain is large, a tiny difference between the inputs makes a big swing at the output, until that swing runs into a supply rail and clips. Switch tiers to see it as a schematic, as a spool valve the two inputs push on, and as the real silicon underneath.
